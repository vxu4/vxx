A p o p h e n i a

Interactive projection using Kinect, Unity, and projector. Exhibited at the RISD museum.

Apophenia is the tendency to see patterns, connections, and meanings among unconnected, random information or data. This interactive projection is one part of a three part exhibition series examining the non-neutrality in random data and patterns–we often bring our own lens and subjectivities, seeing patterns, connections, and metaphors that may or may not be there. This interactive projection piece creates a collaborative digital painting from participants’ movements. A sensor translates gesture to gravitational force — moving and tracing words across a virtual landscape. The traced landscape is a unique composition of each view, of each person's subjective vision and experience. The words are drawn from Hito Steyerl’s “A Sea of Data”, a piece delving into the patterns and stereotypes too often read into data and still tagged as objective.
